Newport News VA's Expert Asphalt Paving Contractor

Newport News is one of Virginia's largest and most dynamic cities — stretching from the historic shipbuilding district along the James River waterfront to the thriving commercial corridor of Oyster Point and the residential sprawl of Denbigh and Kiln Creek. As a Hampton Roads city on the coastal plain, Newport News presents distinct paving conditions that differ from the Piedmont region around our Chester VA home base. Sandy tidal soils, higher groundwater levels, humidity from the James River and Hampton Roads, and salt-air exposure in waterfront neighborhoods all affect how asphalt paving must be approached to deliver long-lasting results.

J. Worden & Sons brings 40+ years of paving expertise to Newport News projects. We understand that proper base preparation is especially critical in coastal plain soils that don't compact the same way Piedmont clay does. Our approach includes thorough site evaluation, engineered drainage planning for waterfront and low-lying properties, quality asphalt mixes appropriate for the coastal environment, and sealcoating recommendations calibrated to Newport News's humidity and salt exposure. From residential driveways in Hilton Village and Denbigh to commercial parking lots in the Oyster Point and Jefferson Avenue corridor, we deliver the same 4th-generation quality our customers in Central Virginia have trusted since 1984.

How does Newport News's coastal climate affect asphalt paving?

Newport News's position on the James River waterfront and proximity to Hampton Roads introduces salt-air exposure and higher humidity than our inland Central Virginia service area. These factors accelerate asphalt oxidation and make proper sealcoating especially important. We use quality sealcoating materials, thorough drainage engineering, and recommend a 2–3 year sealcoating interval for waterfront and near-water properties in Newport News.
