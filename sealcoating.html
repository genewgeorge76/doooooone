Asphalt Sealcoating — Richmond VA & Central Virginia

Sealcoating is the single most cost-effective asphalt maintenance service available. Virginia's climate delivers a full assault on asphalt year-round: intense UV radiation that oxidizes the binder, heavy rainfall that infiltrates cracks and undermines the base, automotive fluids that dissolve unprotected surfaces, and freeze-thaw cycles that widen every crack. A professional sealcoat addresses all of these simultaneously for a fraction of the cost of repair or repaving.

J. Worden & Sons applies coal tar and asphalt emulsion sealers depending on the application. Our process begins with thorough surface cleaning — blowing off debris, treating oil spots with primer, filling cracks larger than 1/8 inch. We mask off edges, apply sealer at proper coverage rates (50–75 sq ft/gallon), and optionally apply a second coat for maximum protection. Properly applied sealcoating cures in 24–48 hours and provides 3–5 years of protection.

Asphalt Sealcoating — Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I sealcoat my driveway in Virginia?

Every 3–5 years. Sealing annually causes buildup; too infrequently allows UV oxidation to accumulate. If the surface looks gray and oxidized rather than black, it's time.

How much does sealcoating cost in Richmond VA?

$0.15–$0.25 per sq ft. A 600 sq ft driveway costs $90–$150. Minimum charges apply for small jobs.

How long does sealcoating take to dry?

24–48 hours. No vehicle traffic for 24 hours minimum. Don't sealcoat if rain is forecast within 24 hours.

When can new asphalt be sealcoated?

6–12 months after paving. Sealing too soon traps oils and prevents proper curing.

Do you fill cracks before sealcoating?

Yes — crack filling before sealing is included in our full-service process. Sealing over open cracks is only cosmetic.

Do you serve Williamsburg and New Kent for sealcoating?

Yes — sealcoating service extends to Williamsburg VA, New Kent County, and all communities along the I-64 corridor.
