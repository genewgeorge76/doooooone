Why Asphalt? Richmond's Climate & Soil

Richmond's unique conditions demand flexible pavement. Our hot, humid summers and occasional ice storms create freeze-thaw cycles that crack rigid concrete. Asphalt's flexibility handles this movement without failing. Additionally, Richmond's clay-heavy soils expand when wet and shrink when dry. A properly prepared asphalt surface on a compacted aggregate base accommodates this movement—rigid concrete does not.

Result: Asphalt costs 30–40% less than concrete upfront, handles Virginia's seasonal demands better, and is easier to repair when needed.

The Driveway Installation Process

Step 1: Free On-Site Consultation

We visit your property, evaluate existing conditions, measure the area, and provide a detailed written estimate.

Step 2: Site Preparation & Excavation

Excavate to 8–10 inches in Richmond's clay soils. Remove unsuitable material. Ensure proper grading for water drainage—water is asphalt's enemy.

Step 3: Base Installation & Compaction

Install 4–6 inches of compacted crushed stone aggregate base. Heavy rollers compact to maximum density. This foundation is where cheap jobs fail.

Step 4: Asphalt Application

Premium hot-mix asphalt laid at ~300°F. Spread to 2–3 inches for driveways. Smooth, professional application.

Step 5: Rolling & Compaction

Steel drum and pneumatic rollers eliminate air voids and create a smooth, durable surface.

Step 6: Final Walkthrough & Cleanup

Inspect finished surface. Walk the project with you. Complete site cleanup.

Driveway Lifespan & Maintenance

A properly installed asphalt driveway lasts 20–30 years in Richmond's climate. Extend that lifespan with:

  • Sealcoating: Every 3–5 years (blocks UV, water, oil damage)
  • Crack sealing: Address cracks immediately, especially before winter
  • Drainage: Keep gutters clear so water drains away from pavement
  • Avoid salt: Richmond rarely needs de-icing, but minimize salt use if needed
