Site Grading & Excavation — Richmond VA & Central Virginia

Your asphalt is only as good as the base underneath it. Proper grading and excavation — removing existing material, shaping the sub-grade, installing a compacted aggregate base at the correct depth and grade — separates a driveway that lasts 25 years from one that fails in five. J. Worden & Sons has been performing professional site preparation across Richmond VA and Central Virginia since 1984, with equipment and expertise for everything from residential driveways to large commercial site work.

Virginia's clay-heavy soils require special attention. Expansive clay absorbs moisture and swells, then shrinks in dry periods — destabilizing any surface above it if not properly addressed. Our process: survey the site, establish drainage grades, excavate to required depth (8–10 inches in Chesterfield and Henrico County clay areas), import and compact VDOT-spec crushed aggregate base in lifts, proof-roll before any asphalt is applied.

Site Grading & Excavation — Frequently Asked Questions

Why is proper grading important for asphalt?

Correct grading ensures water drains away rather than pooling or infiltrating the base. Water is asphalt's #1 enemy — it softens the base, enables freeze-thaw damage, and accelerates cracking.

How deep do you excavate for a driveway in Richmond VA?

8–10 inches for residential driveways in Virginia clay areas — allowing 4–6 inches of compacted aggregate base plus 2–3 inches of asphalt.

Can you fix drainage problems with regrading?

Yes — regrading to correct drainage is one of our specialties. We can reshape grades, add catch basins, and redirect drainage channels to eliminate ponding.

What base material do you use?

VDOT-specified #21A or #21B crushed aggregate, compacted in 4–6 inch lifts to maximum density.
