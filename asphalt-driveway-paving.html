Asphalt Driveway Paving — Richmond VA & Central Virginia

A new asphalt driveway is one of the best investments a Richmond homeowner can make — adding curb appeal, protecting your property, and lasting 20–30 years with proper care. J. Worden & Sons has been installing and replacing driveways across Richmond, Chester, Chesterfield, Henrico, Short Pump, Ashland, New Kent, and Williamsburg since 1984. Our process begins with a thorough site evaluation: soil conditions, drainage slopes, existing base integrity, and excavation depth requirements — before a single shovel touches the ground.

Every driveway installation includes excavation to 8–10 inches in Virginia's clay soils, removal of all existing material, a properly graded and compacted crushed aggregate base, and 2–3 inches of premium hot-mix asphalt laid and compacted to a smooth professional finish. We clean the site completely. The result handles Virginia's freeze-thaw cycles and decades of vehicle traffic without premature failure.

Asphalt Driveway Paving — Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new asphalt driveway cost in Richmond VA?

$3–$7 per square foot installed. A 2-car driveway (600 sq ft) costs $1,800–$4,200. Call (804) 446-1296 for a free in-person estimate.

How long does an asphalt driveway last in Virginia?

20–30 years with sealcoating every 3–5 years. Proper 8–10 inch excavation and compacted base is the critical factor — it's where most cheap jobs fail.

How long before I can drive on my new driveway?

24–48 hours for light traffic, 72 hours full use. Avoid turning wheels while stationary for the first week.

Do you remove the old driveway?

Yes — full removal and disposal included at no extra charge.

Can you widen my existing driveway?

Yes — widening and extensions are a common service. We match the existing grade and tie seamlessly into the current edge.

Do you serve Williamsburg and Ashland?

Yes — we serve the full Central Virginia region including Williamsburg VA, Ashland VA, New Kent County, and all communities to Richmond's east and north.
