Colonial Heights VA's Reliable Asphalt Paving Contractor

Colonial Heights is a small independent city tucked directly south of Petersburg, defined by its strong Route 1 commercial corridor, established mid-century residential neighborhoods, and the quiet pride its residents take in their properties. Many of Colonial Heights' subdivisions — from the Conduit Road area to Walkerton and the neighborhoods around Colonial Heights High School — were built in the 1950s through 1980s. Driveways laid in those decades are now reaching or exceeding the end of their serviceable life, showing cracking, heaving, and alligatoring that signals it's time for professional attention.

J. Worden & Sons has been the go-to paving contractor for homeowners and businesses throughout the Colonial Heights and greater Petersburg area since 1984. We understand the Piedmont clay soils common to this area — soils that expand when wet and shrink when dry, creating the seasonal pressure cycles that crack asphalt from beneath. Our base preparation process removes problematic subsoil as needed, installs a properly compacted aggregate base with correct drainage slopes, and delivers an asphalt surface engineered to outlast cheaper alternatives by a decade or more. Whether you have a residential driveway that needs replacing on Conduit Road or a commercial lot on Boulevard requiring new paving and ADA-compliant striping, we have the crew and equipment to handle it right.

Asphalt Paving Colonial Heights VA — FAQs

How much does a driveway paving cost in Colonial Heights VA?

Driveway paving in Colonial Heights VA typically costs $3–$7 per square foot. A standard two-car driveway (600–800 sq ft) runs approximately $2,500–$6,000. We travel from Chester VA — roughly 30 miles — with no travel surcharge for Colonial Heights jobs. All estimates are free, written, and in-person. Call (804) 446-1296 to schedule your visit.

My Colonial Heights driveway is 30+ years old — repair or replace?

A driveway that's 30+ years old has typically reached the end of its serviceable life in Colonial Heights. When more than 25–30% of the surface shows cracking, alligatoring, or structural failure, full replacement is usually more cost-effective than patching. We assess each driveway individually — if the existing base is still sound, an overlay may work. We give you an honest recommendation in writing with no pressure either way.

Do you serve the Route 1 corridor in Colonial Heights for commercial paving?

Yes — we handle commercial parking lots, driveways, and surface paving along the Route 1 corridor in Colonial Heights and surrounding commercial areas on Temple Avenue and Boulevard. Services include full parking lot installation, ADA-compliant striping, drainage engineering, and phased construction to keep your business operational. We spec the appropriate asphalt thickness for Route 1's high-traffic commercial demands.

What's the best time of year to pave in Colonial Heights?

Asphalt paving in Colonial Heights works best when pavement temperatures are above 50°F — typically April through October in Central Virginia. Spring (April–May) and fall (September–October) offer ideal conditions: moderate temperatures, good compaction, and less rain risk. Summer works well too, though afternoon heat can affect scheduling. We avoid paving in freezing conditions. Call early in spring as schedules fill quickly during peak season.

Do you do line striping and ADA compliance for Colonial Heights parking lots?

Yes — line striping and ADA compliance are integral to our commercial paving service in Colonial Heights. We stripe standard parking spaces, fire lanes, handicap-accessible spaces with correct dimensions, crosswalks, loading zones, and directional arrows. ADA requirements include proper space widths, access aisles, and slope specifications. Striping is also available as a standalone service to refresh existing lots without full repaving.
