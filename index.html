Complete Guide

Asphalt Paving in Richmond VA — Everything You Need to Know

Why Richmond VA Homeowners Choose Asphalt Paving

Richmond, Virginia's unique climate and soil conditions make asphalt paving one of the most practical and cost-effective solutions for driveways, parking lots, and private roads throughout the metro area. Hot, humid summers, occasional ice storms, and freeze-thaw cycles demand a pavement material that flexes rather than cracks. Asphalt's inherent flexibility — combined with proper base preparation — handles Virginia's seasonal demands far better than rigid concrete.

The Richmond area's clay-heavy soils present additional challenges: they expand when wet and shrink when dry. A properly prepared asphalt surface on a compacted aggregate base accommodates this movement. This is why J. Worden & Sons has specialized in asphalt paving throughout Central Virginia — including Richmond, Chester, Chesterfield, Henrico, Short Pump, Midlothian, Ashland, New Kent, and Williamsburg — for over 40 years.

Our Asphalt Paving Process: From Start to Finish

Step 1: Free On-Site Consultation. We visit your property, evaluate existing conditions, measure the area, and provide a detailed written estimate — all at no cost or obligation.

Step 2: Site Preparation & Excavation. We excavate to the appropriate depth (8–12 inches), remove unsuitable material, and ensure proper grading for water drainage.

Step 3: Base Installation & Compaction. A compacted aggregate base (4–6 inches of crushed stone) provides the structural foundation. We compact using heavy rollers to maximum density.

Step 4: Asphalt Application. Premium hot-mix asphalt is laid at approximately 300°F and spread to specified thickness — 2–3 inches for driveways, 3–4 inches for commercial lots.

Step 5: Rolling & Compaction. Steel drum and pneumatic rollers compact the asphalt, eliminating air voids and creating a smooth, durable surface.

Step 6: Final Walkthrough. We inspect the finished surface and walk the project with you before we leave.

Residential vs. Commercial Asphalt Paving

Residential driveways require 2–3 inches of asphalt on a 4–6 inch base. Commercial parking lots need 3–4 inches minimum, engineered drainage, ADA-compliant design, and proper load capacity for delivery trucks and heavy vehicles. J. Worden & Sons handles both — residential driveways in neighborhoods like Tuckahoe, Sleepy Hollow, Windsor Farms, and Westham Parkway, and commercial lots along Midlothian Turnpike, Hull Street, West Broad Street, and the Route 60 corridor in Williamsburg.

Maintaining Your Asphalt in Virginia's Climate

Sealcoating: Every 3–5 years to block UV, water, and chemical damage. Wait 6–12 months after new paving. Learn about our sealcoating service →

Crack Sealing: Address cracks immediately — especially before winter. Learn about asphalt repair →

Drainage: Keep gutters clear so water drains away from pavement. Standing water is asphalt's worst enemy.

Asphalt Paving Questions & Answers

How much does asphalt driveway paving cost in Richmond VA?

Asphalt driveway paving in Richmond VA typically costs $3–$7 per square foot installed. A standard 2-car driveway (600 sq ft) runs $1,800–$4,200. Commercial lots run $2–$5/sq ft. Factors affecting cost include soil conditions, excavation depth, existing material removal, and site access. J. Worden & Sons provides free, written, in-person estimates. Call (804) 446-1296.

How long does an asphalt driveway last in Virginia?

A properly installed asphalt driveway in Virginia lasts 20–30 years with sealcoating every 3–5 years. Virginia's clay soils and seasonal freeze-thaw cycles require 8–10 inch excavation depth and a compacted aggregate base. Driveways that fail early almost always have inadequate base preparation.

When is the best time to pave in Richmond VA?

Late March through November when temperatures stay above 50°F. Spring and fall are ideal — moderate temps and lower humidity. We book quickly in May–June, so schedule your free estimate early.

What is sealcoating and how often should it be done?

Sealcoating is a protective layer (coal tar or asphalt emulsion) applied every 3–5 years. It blocks UV radiation, water infiltration, and oil spills. New asphalt should cure 6–12 months before first application. Regular sealcoating extends pavement life by 50%+.

Is asphalt better than concrete for Virginia driveways?

Asphalt costs 30–40% less upfront than concrete, handles Virginia's freeze-thaw cycles better due to flexibility, and is easier to repair. Concrete lasts longer but costs more. Most Richmond VA homeowners choose asphalt for the best overall value.
